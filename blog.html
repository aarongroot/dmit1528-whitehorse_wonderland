Maclean's Insider Whitehorse Guide

December 1, 2023

Maclean’s magazine has just dropped a great new article called “An insider’s guide to epic winter exploration in the Yukon" created by Travel Yukon and you should definitely check it out. A link to the article will be provided at the end of this post. The article serves as an invitation to discover the wonders of Canada’s “Land of the Midnight Sun”. This article showcases the awe that comes from witnessing the northern lights, which is even more amazing right now due to the current solar maximum which only occurs every 11 years!

The article also showcases that Whitehorse is more than just place to watch the aurora borealis, emphasizing not only its vibrant culture but also the multiple thrilling outdoor activities. This includes pursuits such as dogsledding, snowshoe hiking, and snowmobiling to reveal the territory's commitment to embracing the winter wonderland season. The abundant amount of cross-country skiing will also greatly appeal to the outdoor winter enthusiasts.

The inclusion of winter festivals such as Rendezvous, which is a weeklong celebration to celebrate all aspects of the Yukon’s rich culture and history, adds depth to the entire Whitehorse experience. During events such as this, you watch people throw axes and even chainsaws!

If you are planning a trip up to this winter wilderness this article provides valuable information to help conquer all the activities you may choose. From recommended activities during the winter solstice to safety precautions when venturing out to witness the northern lights, this article ensures that readers are well-prepared for their Yukon exploration.

In conclusion this article showcases that Whitehorse is more than just a city up North in Canada. There is so many different opportunities to experience something truly awe-inspiring. It can be very cold and the days may be short but Whitehorse is an experience that everyone needs to experience.
